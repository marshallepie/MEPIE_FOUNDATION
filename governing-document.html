Governing Document

Our charitable constitution - the foundation of everything we do

About This Document

The Governing Document is the Charity's constitution—setting out our charitable objects, governance structure, trustee responsibilities, and operational rules. It is the legal framework that governs all our activities.

Legal Status

Registered with the Charity Commission for England and Wales

Structure

Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO) - Foundation Model

Governing Law

Law of England and Wales

Key Sections

1. Name and Registered Status

The charity is named MEPIE Foundation and is registered as a Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO) with the Charity Commission for England and Wales.

2. Objects (Charitable Purposes)

Primary Purpose: To advance education, skills development, and technical training for the public benefit, particularly in underserved and developing communities, including but not limited to Cameroon.

How We Achieve This:

  • Establishing and supporting technical and vocational education initiatives
  • Providing access to practical, technology-focused learning environments
  • Supporting infrastructure, equipment, and training resources
  • Promoting self-sufficiency, employability, and sustainable economic development

Initial flagship project: ME Technical School in Yaounde, Cameroon

3. Powers

To further its objects, the Charity may:

  • Raise funds and receive donations, grants, and sponsorship
  • Purchase, lease, or use land, buildings, and equipment
  • Employ staff or engage volunteers and contractors
  • Enter into lawful agreements and partnerships
  • Invest funds prudently in accordance with charity law
  • Do anything lawful and necessary to further its charitable purposes

4. Trustees

The Charity shall have no fewer than three trustees. The initial trustees are:

  • Marshall Epie
  • Aruna Ramineni
  • Fitzroy Shrowder

Trustees are jointly and equally responsible. There are no "silent" trustees.

5. Trustee Meetings

Frequency

At least twice per year. May meet more frequently as needed.

Quorum

Two trustees or one-third of total, whichever is greater.

Decisions

Consensus preferred; simple majority vote when required.

6. Trustee Appointments and Stepping Down

Appointment of New Trustees

New trustees may be appointed by a majority decision of existing trustees. All trustees must be eligible under charity law.

Resignation / Stepping Down

A trustee may step down by giving written notice to the Charity, with resignation taking effect on receipt or a specified later date.

A trustee automatically ceases to be a trustee if they:

  • Become disqualified under charity law
  • Are absent from trustee meetings for 12 months without good reason
  • Act in a way that seriously damages the Charity or breaches trustee duties

Automatic Cessation of Trusteeship

A trustee shall automatically cease to hold office if they:

  • die;
  • become incapable by reason of mental or physical illness or injury of managing their own affairs;
  • become disqualified from acting as a trustee under charity law; or
  • are absent from trustee meetings for a continuous period of twelve months without good reason and without the consent of the other trustees.

7. Conflicts of Interest

Trustees must declare any personal or financial interest in matters discussed. A trustee with a conflict must not vote on the affected decision. All conflicts must be recorded in the minutes.

8. Financial Management

Bank Account

  • Maintained with NatWest Bank
  • All income and expenditure passes through this account
  • At least two trustees are authorized signatories

Transparency

  • Monthly bookkeeping entries maintained
  • Financial summaries published on website
  • Annual accounts prepared as required

9. Amendments to the Governing Document

This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority of trustees. Amendments must continue to support the Charity's objects, comply with charity law, and receive any required approvals from the Charity Commission.

10. Dissolution

If the Charity is dissolved, all remaining assets must be transferred to another charity with similar charitable purposes, as approved by the trustees and compliant with charity law. Trustees may not benefit personally from dissolution.

11. Governing Law

This constitution is governed by the law of England and Wales.

12. Trustee Benefits and Payments

Trustees shall not receive any payment for acting as trustees.

Trustees may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses properly incurred when acting on behalf of the Charity, with appropriate supporting documentation required.

13. Investments

The trustees may invest the funds of the Charity in any lawful manner, provided that investments are made prudently, in the best interests of the Charity, with reasonable risk levels, and do not conflict with the Charity's charitable purposes.

14. Land and Property

The Charity may acquire, hold, lease, and dispose of land or property in furtherance of its charitable purposes. All decisions relating to land or property shall be made collectively by the trustees and recorded in the Charity's minutes.

Declaration

We, the initial trustees of MEPIE Foundation, adopt this governing document as the constitution of the Charity.
