6. Trustee Meetings
6.1 Frequency

6.2 Quorum
A quorum is two trustees or one-third of the total number, whichever is greater.

6.3 Decision-Making and Voting
Decisions should be made by consensus where possible.
Where a vote is required:
Each trustee has one vote
Decisions are passed by a simple majority
In the event of a tie, the Chair has a casting vote
Meetings may be held in person or electronically.

7. Trustee Appointments and Stepping Down
7.1 Appointment of New Trustees
New trustees may be appointed by a majority decision of existing trustees.
All trustees must be eligible under charity law.

7.2 Resignation / Stepping Down
A trustee may step down by:
Giving written notice to the Charity
Resignation taking effect on receipt or a specified later date
A trustee automatically ceases to be a trustee if they:
Become disqualified under charity law
Are absent from trustee meetings for 12 months without good reason
Act in a way that seriously damages the Charity or breaches trustee duties
7.3 Automatic Cessation of Trusteeship
A trustee shall automatically cease to hold office if they:
a) die;b) become incapable by reason of mental or physical illness or injury of managing their own affairs;c) become disqualified from acting as a trustee under charity law; ord) are absent from trustee meetings for a continuous period of twelve months without good reason and without the consent of the other trustees.


8. Conflicts of Interest
Trustees must declare any personal or financial interest in matters discussed
A trustee with a conflict must not vote on the affected decision
