Governance & Trustees

Transparent leadership, clear accountability, and full regulatory compliance

Our Governance Principles

As a Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO), we are committed to the highest standards of governance, transparency, and accountability.

Accountability

Trustees are jointly and equally responsible. There are no "silent" trustees.

Transparency

All decisions, conflicts, and financial activities are documented and publicly available.

Public Benefit

Every decision prioritizes the Charity's objects and serves the public good.

Trustee Responsibilities

Our trustees are collectively responsible for ensuring the charity operates legally, ethically, and effectively in pursuit of its charitable objects.

Strategic Oversight

  • Acting only in the best interests of the Charity
  • Ensuring the Charity carries out its purposes for public benefit
  • Setting strategy and overseeing operations
  • Appointing and supervising staff or volunteers where applicable

Legal Compliance

  • Complying with charity law, financial regulations, and the constitution
  • Managing resources responsibly and safeguarding assets
  • Ensuring proper financial controls and reporting
  • Maintaining accurate records and transparent reporting

No Silent Trustees

All trustees are jointly and equally responsible. There are no "silent" trustees. Each trustee is actively engaged in governance, decision-making, and oversight of the charity's activities.

How We Make Decisions

Meeting Frequency

Trustees meet at least twice per year, as required by our governing document and charity law.

During the Foundation's early years, meeting every 6 months is acceptable and compliant. Trustees may meet more frequently if required by project activity, funding needs, or risk management.

Quorum

A quorum is two trustees or one-third of the total number, whichever is greater.

This ensures that no decisions can be made without adequate representation and discussion among trustees.

Voting Process

Consensus First

Decisions should be made by consensus where possible, ensuring all trustees are heard.

Majority Vote

Where a vote is required, each trustee has one vote, and decisions pass by simple majority.

Casting Vote

In the event of a tie, the Chair has a casting vote to ensure decisions can move forward.

Meetings may be held in person or electronically to ensure flexibility and accessibility.

Conflicts of Interest Policy

We maintain strict policies to ensure all decisions are made in the charity's best interests, free from personal gain.

1. Declaration

Trustees must declare any personal or financial interest in matters discussed at meetings.

2. Exclusion

A trustee with a conflict must not vote on the affected decision.

3. Documentation

All conflicts must be recorded in the minutes of trustee meetings.

Trustee Benefits and Payments

Trustees shall not receive any payment for acting as trustees. This ensures that all decisions are made purely in the charity's interests, without financial motivation.

Trustees may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses properly incurred when acting on behalf of the Charity, with appropriate supporting documentation required.

Financial Oversight

Trustees ensure all financial activities are conducted transparently, legally, and in line with charity best practices.

Bank Account Management

  • Single charity bank account with NatWest Bank
  • All income and expenditure passes through this account
  • At least two trustees are authorized signatories
  • No personal use of charity funds

Financial Controls

  • Accurate and up-to-date financial records
  • All spending authorized and traceable
  • Funds used solely for charitable purposes
  • Monthly bookkeeping published transparently
